Artist Statement

Painting the wild heart of Maine

Katie Foster is a Maine-based painter whose work is rooted in deep observation of the natural world. Working primarily in oils, she seeks to capture the fleeting quality of light as it moves across the coast, forest, and bog — moments that are as much felt as seen.

Her paintings begin outdoors, in direct conversation with the landscape. Back in the studio, she distills those experiences into compositions that balance the specific and the universal — the particular cove at low tide, the exact quality of a late-summer evening — while remaining open to what the paint itself wants to do.

Foster is drawn to the edges of things: where land meets water, where summer becomes fall, where a path disappears into the trees. Maine offers an inexhaustible supply of such thresholds, and she considers herself a faithful student of the place she calls home.

"I'm interested in the moment just before something changes — the light before it shifts, the season on the turn. Painting is my way of staying in that moment a little longer."
